Updates
 

Game & Profile Review — Interested in having your games and training habits analyzed by one of our senseis? Join the Game & Profile Review! You'll get placed with a cohort of similarly-rated players. Each week, you meet with your peers and analyze one member's game. A few days later, you meet with the sensei, who reviews the same game and answers questions from your peer review session. The sensei will also look at your profile and training habits!

Recent Content
 

Bishop Odds vs 1600

In this bishop-odds game, IM David Pruess must fend off an aggressive attack from his 1600 opponent. The game shows why material odds lead to sharper, more instructive, and more exciting games than equal-material mismatches.

The Problem with Chessbrah Habits

GM Kraai discusses the Chessbrah “Habits” series, arguing that blitz-focused rules and shortcuts may give short-term results but ultimately prevent real improvement.

Geller's Application of Chess Theory

Geller’s Application of Chess Theory isn’t just a historical classic — it’s a powerful training tool for learning how to evaluate positions without engine reliance. In this review, Jesse explores Geller’s ideas and his wins over Fischer.

Queen Odds vs 1100

In this queen odds game against an 1100-rated opponent, David slowly recovers material by focusing on space, piece activity, and avoiding unnecessary trades. The game highlights how creating pressure and confusion can be more effective than playing for simple clarity—even when down major material.

Inside the Mind of Fischer with IM John Donaldson

John Donaldson joins Dojo Talks to discuss his new book "Inside the Mind of Bobby Fischer", a collection of Fischer’s lesser-known writings and annotations.

Queen of Chess

In this episode of Dojo Talks, the sensei review Netflix's documentary Queen of Chess and discuss Judit Polgar’s rise to the top of chess. They break down what the film gets right, what it leaves out, and what Judit's legacy means for future players.
